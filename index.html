Capybaras are the largest living rodents, native to South America, and are closely related to guinea pigs. They are highly social, semi-aquatic animals that live in groups near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. With webbed feet and dense fur, they are excellent swimmers and can stay submerged for several minutes to evade predators. Capybaras are herbivores, feeding mainly on grasses and aquatic plants. Known for their calm and tolerant temperament, they coexist peacefully with many other animal species.
